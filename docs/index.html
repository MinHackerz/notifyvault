1. Information Collection & Access

NotifyVault uses the Android NotificationListenerService permission to capture and save incoming system notifications (such as Title, Text, Subtext, App Name, Package Name, and timestamp) directly into a local SQLite database on your device.

Zero-Data Sharing: All notification logs are stored strictly on your local device. We do not maintain cloud servers, and we never collect, upload, share, or transmit your database logs to any external systems or third parties.

2. Advertisements

To support ongoing development, NotifyVault displays third-party advertisements using the Google Mobile Ads SDK (AdMob).

Google AdMob may collect and process device advertising identifiers (such as the Android Ad ID) and location metrics to serve personalized or non-personalized banner ads. The collection and usage of this data are governed by Google's Privacy Policy.

3. Data Retention and Deletion

Because your data is stored locally, you are in complete control of its lifecycle:

  • Retention Settings: You can configure automatic database cleanups (e.g. automatically purge messages older than 7 days) inside the Settings screen.
  • Manual Wipe: You can erase your entire notification history database instantly by tapping the "Clear Database" button in the Settings menu.
  • App Uninstall: Uninstalling the NotifyVault application from your Android device immediately deletes the entire SQLite database file and all stored logs from your disk permanently.

4. Security

Since your database is offline and stored locally, it is protected by Android’s standard sandboxing architecture. No other applications on your device can access NotifyVault's private database directory.

5. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the updated Privacy Policy page here. You are advised to review this page periodically for any modifications.
